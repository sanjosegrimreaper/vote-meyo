Why we Love Mr. M.!

Students and Mr. Meyerowitz voters explain why Mr. Meyerowitz is special.

From Dora L.

Mr. Meyerowitz brings humor into his lectures, and has a genuine passion with what he’s teaching, making learning about the history of today’s world much more interesting. Contrary to a traditional high school history course, Mr. Meyerowitz sheds light on the current events of the world and connects them to his lectures, while providing his views on the situation, encouraging students to think deeply about what they’ve been learning. Not only that, but he goes out of his way to ensure every student is engaged in the course, and provides many opportunities for his students to engage with fellow classmates and deep class discussion. Mr. Meyerowitz's passion for teaching and his subject expertise really stands out to me!

From Elaine W.

Mr. Meyerowitz is a teacher I greatly look up to. Having this much passion for something is really something I admire. Mr. Meyerowitz definitely takes a lot of time and effort into preparing his lesson plans and just learning about the world around us so that we students can come up to him and ask him questions. I love going into his class every day and look forward to seeing him leaning with his feet against the wall of the doorway, greeting us as we come in. I look forward to seeing the tie he wears and how it matches the lesson we’ll learn today. He loves bombarding us with words that help us understand ideas and concepts and makes sure to follow up on what we covered in class with his word-abundant announcements. I truly love being in his class and having his sound frequencies propagate into my ear.

From Timothy D.

The feeling Mr. Meyerowitz gives his students can be felt with a step into his Room 236– a world of color, a world of passion. It’s that passion that I can feel, even aside from class time, in those few minutes in passing period (check out on YT!). Any question leads to engaging conversations that capture our deepest intellectual ambitions. Even after club time, which he graciously helps me advise, you can always see him talking with someone about their curious questions, even as the clock strikes past closing. He holds an infectious passion that brings color to everyone around them; honestly, you can't find a better teacher to be the face of America!

From Iris L.

Since the first time I met him in 9th grade, Mr. Meyerowitz has left a lasting impression on me. He has always been very supportive not only in class, but outside of class with my other clubs such as The Scope! As someone who is very focused in STEM, I'm not as strong in the Humanities, but at times his class CoPoco becomes the highlight of my day. For example, I really love close reading days when we can draw posters on recent topics.

From Evelyn W.

Mr. Meyerowitz is probably one of if not the most passionate teacher out there. Sometimes I genuinely feel like this guy is a machine. How does he not get tired of all this history? Not only does he treat every lecture like an exciting field trip to a new site of colonialism, he also actively seeks new knowledge to spread to us outside of class. Just look at the five paragraph essays he posts every week on Schoology. It's probably longer than my close readings. He treats every one of our assignments like a battlefield; he meticulously goes through our work like he's getting paid a million dollars per assignment (he's not), all for the sake of his students' improvement. His criticism is kind and constructive, the type that makes me want to throw everything else down to continue my project in his class. All in all, his passion is both contagious and eye-opening (physically and metaphorically).
