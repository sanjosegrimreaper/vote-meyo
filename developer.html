From the Developer

A message from the developer, about why Mr. Meyerowitz deserves to win.

Mr. Meyerowitz is the kind of teacher who makes you actually want to show up. Not because you have to, but because you know whatever he's got planned is going to be worth it. I've never once walked out of his class feeling like I just sat through another period. Every single day, you can tell he cares about what we're doing, and that energy is contagious.

His Colonial and Post-Colonial History class changed the way I think. Not just about history, but about everything. We don't just memorize dates and events. We question things. We pull apart perspectives, challenge each other, and connect the past to what's happening right now. That's what makes it feel real instead of like something trapped in a textbook.

The debates are honestly my favorite part. I've never been in a class where people genuinely care about what they're saying. It's not about participation points or prepping for an AP exam. People actually listen to each other, push back on ideas, and walk away thinking differently. That kind of thing doesn't just happen on its own.

There's this camaraderie in the room that's hard to explain unless you've felt it. We're all figuring things out together, not competing, not performing, just learning. That environment is rare and it comes from the culture he set from day one.

Earlier this year, he went through something really difficult on a personal level. I'm not going to get into specifics because that's his business, but he never stopped showing up for us. Same energy. Same honesty. He kept posting his funny Schoology updates (if you know, you know), kept recording lectures so none of us fell behind, and still made class feel worth being in. That says a lot about who he is beyond just being a teacher.

Mr. Meyerowitz taught me how to think critically, how to question things, and what it looks like to actually care about what you do. I'm a better student because of him, and I know I'm not the only one.

So if you've read this far, vote for him. Teachers like him don't come around often, and this is our chance to show him it doesn't go unnoticed.

From Aryav K.
